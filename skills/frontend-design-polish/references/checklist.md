# Frontend Design Polish Checklist

## Hierarchy
- Can the user identify the page title, current context, primary action, and secondary information within 3 seconds?
- Are headings, body copy, captions, and metadata clearly separated by weight, size, and color?
- Does the visual order match the product priority?

## Layout And Spacing
- Is there a clear grid or alignment rhythm?
- Are related elements grouped tightly and unrelated elements separated enough?
- Does the page feel dense in a controlled way rather than cramped?

## Typography
- Is the font choice consistent with the product tone?
- Are line length, line height, and heading scale comfortable?
- Are there too many font sizes or weights?

## Color And Surfaces
- Is there one clear accent strategy instead of many competing accents?
- Do surfaces have enough contrast to separate sections without becoming noisy?
- Are borders, fills, and shadows doing distinct jobs?

## Components
- Is the primary CTA visually dominant?
- Do pills, badges, cards, and inputs feel like one family?
- Are hover, active, selected, disabled, and loading states coherent?

## Motion
- Is motion used to explain state changes, not just decorate them?
- Would removing an animation hurt comprehension? If not, simplify or remove it.

## Responsive Behavior
- Does the page still read clearly on narrower widths?
- Do key sections wrap cleanly without creating awkward voids or overflow?

## Finish
- What is the single most generic-looking area?
- What is the single most premium-looking area?
- Can the page be improved with one less color, one less border, or one stronger type decision?
