# Design Critique Heuristics

## Hierarchy
- Do I know where to look first?
- Is the primary action visually obvious?
- Are supporting details quieter than decisions and outcomes?

## Clarity
- Does the interface explain itself through structure, or only through explanatory copy?
- Are repeated controls or labels creating noise?

## Density
- Is this dense because the product is powerful, or dense because nothing was grouped?
- Is whitespace creating order or wasting space?

## Brand Signal
- Does this feel like a real product with a point of view?
- What makes it look generic?

## Perceived Quality
- Are corners, borders, shadows, and fills consistent?
- Does typography feel deliberate, or just default?
- Are surfaces layered with purpose?

## Interaction Confidence
- Can I tell what is clickable?
- Do status, selected, hover, and disabled states feel distinct?

## Critique Vocabulary
- Use words like: compressed, noisy, flat, timid, over-explained, fragmented, heavy, breathless, generic, premium, editorial, sharp, calm, intentional.
- Avoid vague filler like: modern, nicer, cleaner, better UX, more beautiful.
